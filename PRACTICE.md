# Welding Practice — Scrap Inventory & Exercises

Practice stock on hand and what to do with it. The goal is to attempt every
real weld type in the build at least once before touching the real sticks,
and to calibrate heat settings on the actual build wall thickness (0.120").

## Practice exercises

### 1. Beads on flat face — heat calibration

**Stock:** 1.5"×1.5"×0.120" coupons (flat face up)

Run straight beads at different wire speed / voltage combos. Goal: consistent
penetration without burn-through, good tie-in at edges, no cold lap. This is
the reference setting for every real weld — do NOT skip it.

### 2. T-joint (tube to tube)

**Stock:** 1.25"×0.120" or 1"×0.120" coupons

Butt one coupon flat against another's side face. Simulates a horizontal-to-leg
joint. Same 0.120" wall, slightly smaller section (less thermal mass = slightly
harder). Good warm-up before cutting into the 1.5" stock.

### 3. 90° corner joint

**Stock:** 1.5"×1.5"×0.120" coupons, mitered at 45°

Two coupons mitered and welded into an L. Exactly what the end-frame corners
are. Practice getting a tight fit-up at the miter and filling with a fillet.

### 4. 45° miter joint (diagonal simulation)

**Stock:** 1.5"×1.5"×0.120" coupons

Cut a coupon at 45° on both ends, tack it between two stubs. Simulates the
end-frame diagonal landing on a leg / stretcher. Practice consistent gap and
fillet fill at the miter seat.

### 5. 3/8" plate to tube side (plate A weld)

**Stock:** 3/8" plate rectangles (~1.5"×5") + 1.5"×0.120" tube coupons

Weld a plate flat against the side of a tube — this is the plate A weld and
the **hardest real weld in the build**: the thick plate acts as a heat sink,
pulling heat away from the joint. Risk: cold lap on the plate side while
trying not to burn through the tube. **Favor the arc toward the plate.**

### 6. 3/16" plate to tube end (plate B weld)

**Stock:** 3/16" plate pieces (~2.5"×5") + 1.5"×0.120" tube coupons

Weld a plate onto the open end of a tube — full perimeter fillet. This is
exactly the plate B weld (stretcher end). Practice not warping the thin plate
with too much heat.

### 7. Angle to tube (ledger weld)

**Stock:** 1"×1"×1/8" angle pieces + tube coupons

Clamp angle along a tube coupon's side face (simulates welding the shelf
ledger). The angle is thinner than the tube — practice not blowing through
the angle's legs while getting fusion on the tube side.

### 8. Burn-through challenge (0.065" wall)

**Stock:** 9¾" of 1.5"×0.065" tube, 3¾" of 1"×0.065" tube

"Hard mode." If you can tack or fillet on 0.065" without blowing through,
0.120" will feel easy. Expect to fail — that's the point. Try a T-joint or
corner; note what settings were too hot.

### 9. Tapping practice

**Stock:** 3/8" plate offcuts

- **3/8"-16:** drill 5/16", tap. Do 2–3 holes. Feel the quarter-turn-back
  rhythm. Test thread quality with an actual bolt.
- **M12×1.75:** drill 10.2mm (13/32"), tap. Simulates the foot-cap. One hole
  is enough — just confirm the tap + drill combo works in 3/8" plate.

### 10. Drilling practice

**Stock:** 3/16" plate pieces + angle iron pieces

- **7/16" clearance holes in 3/16" plate** — dial in feed pressure and speed.
- **Shelf-screw holes in angle** — center punch and drill clearance holes
  every ~1.5" along a short piece, keeping centered on the horizontal leg.

## Suggested order (easy → hard)

| # | Exercise | Why this order |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Beads on 1.5"×0.120" flat | Calibrate heat — no fit-up to worry about |
| 2 | T-joint on 1"–1.25"×0.120" | Introduce fit-up, same wall, smaller section |
| 3 | 90° corner on 1.5"×0.120" | The real end-frame joint geometry |
| 4 | 45° miter on 1.5"×0.120" | The real diagonal joint |
| 5 | 3/8" plate → tube side | Plate A — heat mismatch (hardest weld) |
| 6 | 3/16" plate → tube end | Plate B — thin plate warping risk |
| 7 | Angle → tube | Ledger — thin material, blow-through risk |
| 8 | Anything on 0.065" tube | Burn-through limit finding |
| 9 | Tapping (3/8"-16, M12) | Bench work — no heat, just feel |
| 10 | Drilling (plate + angle) | Bench work — feed pressure, centering |

---

## Notes

- The 47½" stick is the star — guard it for exercises 1, 3, and 4 (heat cal +
  the two critical joint types). Don't burn all 8 coupons on bead practice.
- The 1.25" and 1" × 0.120" tubes are expendable warm-up stock (exercise 2).
- All tapping/drilling can happen before or after welding — it's bench work
  with no fire risk, good for a separate session.
